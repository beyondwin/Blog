# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The site is for readers who arrive from search, GitHub, shared links, or repeat visits and want a clear place to read longer writing. They may be developers looking for practical engineering notes, readers looking for book reflections, or friends and peers following ideas and travel records.

## Product Purpose

`beyondwin` is a personal archive for development articles, reading reviews, ideas, travel notes, source-grounded analysis, and public memory notes. It turns scattered observations into an owned, searchable, maintainable site where each writing type has a predictable content collection and a polished reading experience.

Success looks like:

- A visitor understands the site's writing lanes within the first screen.
- Long Korean and English posts are comfortable to read on desktop and mobile.
- New post types can be added by creating MDX files with clear frontmatter.
- The visual system feels modern and personal without looking like a generic template.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, observant, exacting.

The site should feel like a field notebook kept by a working developer: quiet enough for concentration, opinionated enough to be memorable, and organized enough that old notes remain useful.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with oversized cards, gradient blobs, and vague value-prop copy.
- Portal-style chrome where the writing competes with widgets, sidebars, and platform navigation.
- Monochrome developer blogs that rely only on code-font minimalism and feel unfinished.
- Magazine tropes such as repeated tiny uppercase eyebrows, decorative drop caps, or ornate serif drama that do not match the author's practical voice.

## Design Principles

- Make writing the product. Navigation, metadata, and visual texture must support reading rather than decorate around it.
- Keep ownership visible. The site should feel like an independent home, not a copied platform theme.
- Separate lanes without fragmenting the archive. Development, reviews, ideas, travel, analysis, and memory should be easy to scan from one system.
- Prefer durable structure over clever one-off pages. Collections, cards, layouts, and tokens should make future publishing cheap.
- Let personality come from hierarchy, color, and content rhythm, not noisy illustration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for all body text and controls. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, reduced-motion preferences, readable line lengths, and responsive layouts down to small mobile screens. Korean and English text should both wrap cleanly without overflow.
